## TL;DR
Google DeepMind introduces **Co-Scientist**, a multi-agent AI system designed to help researchers generate novel scientific hypotheses by synthesizing vast bodies of literature and running rigorous, structured reasoning. Scientists describe it as a transformative tool that compresses months of research into days, produces ideas rigorous enough to test in the lab, and has already contributed to published findings.

## Key Takeaways
- The sheer volume of scientific literature makes it impossible for any individual researcher to keep up; knowledge needed at the frontier doubles roughly every two months.
- Co-Scientist is not a single language model but a **multi-agent system** where AI agents take on specialized roles mimicking a real research team—generating, comparing, ranking, and evolving hypotheses.
- The system can connect facts across previously separate fields, enabling creative, cross-disciplinary breakthroughs.
- In a test prompt about epigenomic aspects of liver fibrosis, Co-Scientist produced hypotheses that the researcher "couldn't find any way to disprove."
- The system can run for days or weeks, testing thousands of hypotheses and reading tens of thousands of papers.
- Researchers report going from processes that would take months down to a day or two.
- Ideas generated by Co-Scientist have already led to **published findings**, with more on the way.
- The tool is described as giving scientists "superpowers"—effectively letting a single researcher deploy the equivalent of 50 scientists in a day.
- DeepMind frames the effort as a natural extension of its long history of work aimed at new scientific discoveries.
- The ultimate goal is to radically accelerate the "clock speed of science" and enable discoveries that were previously not possible or even imagined.
