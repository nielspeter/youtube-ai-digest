## TL;DR
Nate Herk demonstrates how a single, carefully structured "slash goal" prompt given to Claude Fable autonomously orchestrated hundreds of sub-agents to build a complete, functioning business from scratch. In just a few hours, Fable researched a real market problem, designed a brand, built a landing page and product dashboard, and generated launch videos using Nate's HeyGen and 11 Labs API keys. The experiment showcases Fable's ability to act as a high-level orchestrator, delegating complex tasks to cheaper models like Opus and Sonnet to deliver a market-ready business package efficiently.

## Key Takeaways
- **Fable as an Orchestrator:** Fable acts as a manager that plans, delegates, and reviews work rather than building everything itself, spinning up sub-agents (Opus/Sonnet) to handle the heavy lifting.
- **Single Prompt, Massive Output:** Using a `/goal` prompt that references a detailed instruction file bypasses Fable's ~4,000 character limit, enabling a massive, multi-phase autonomous workflow.
- **Multi-Agent Workflows:** The prompt instructed Fable to use parallel researchers, run "tournaments" where agents pitched competing ideas, and deploy "skeptic agents" to adversarially verify every claim.
- **Complete Business Package:** Fable delivered a landing page, a functional product dashboard (Counter Brief), business plan, market research, brand guidelines, and multiple launch videos.
- **Creative Use of APIs:** Fable autonomously discovered and used Nate's HeyGen avatar and 11 Labs voice clone from his `.env` file to generate a personalized "founder message" video.
- **Cost and Token Efficiency:** The run consumed about 500,000 tokens and roughly 30-40% of Nate's 5-hour Fable limit on the $200/month plan, but barely touched his weekly Fable budget because sub-agents ran on Opus/Sonnet.
- **Speed to Market:** What would have taken weeks of manual work was accomplished in 3-4 hours, delivering a V1 business package ready for iteration.
- **Importance of Upfront Idea Validation:** Nate notes that while the build was impressive, he would put more emphasis on stress-testing the idea's viability before building everything out in future runs.
- **Iterative Potential:** The experiment suggests a workflow where a second "run" could feed V1 feedback back into Fable to refine and improve the business package further.
