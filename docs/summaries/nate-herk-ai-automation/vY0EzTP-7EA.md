### Freelance vs. In-House Consulting [03:04](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vY0EzTP-7EA&t=184s)
There are two distinct paths for a Claude AI consultant. The first is the freelance consultant, who enjoys freedom and no income ceiling but faces the chaos of client acquisition and income instability. The second, described as the smarter play for many, is the in-house consultant. Companies already have the AI tools but lack the internal skills to scale them; McKinsey found that only 6% of companies are "high performers" with AI. The in-house route offers a stable paycheck and a unique advantage: the consultant already understands the business's people, systems, and wasted time.

### The AI Skills Premium [04:06](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vY0EzTP-7EA&t=246s)
The demand for AI skills has outpaced traditional IT and engineering. Manpower Group data shows AI skills are the hardest to hire for globally. Consequently, PwC's AI Jobs Barometer reports that workers with AI skills earn a 62% wage premium over those without, a number that has increased every year. The central thesis is to "become the AI person" wherever you are, as nobody has a 10-year head start in this nascent field.

### Step 1: Pick One Painful Problem [05:50](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vY0EzTP-7EA&t=350s)
The first step to becoming an AI consultant is to pick one painful problem. List every manual task from the previous week and circle the one that eats the most hours. The key is to adopt a consultant mindset: decide on the specific metric you want to move before building anything. The metric will always be time saved, mistakes cut, or money made. The speaker also gives a quick warning to only use company-approved AI tools and never input sensitive data into unapproved systems.

### Step 2: Build the Fix and Document It [06:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vY0EzTP-7EA&t=398s)
Building the fix doesn't necessarily mean writing code. It involves setting up Claude with the right instructions, examples, templates, and real documents behind the task. You work with Claude until it reliably completes the task, building skills and instructions around the process. Once successful, you record a quick 2-minute before-and-after demo video showing the time savings.

### Step 3: Deliver and Prove the Number Moved [07:08](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vY0EzTP-7EA&t=428s)
Step three is about proving the metric you identified in step one actually moved. For example, show that a 4-hour task now takes 20 minutes, returning 3.5 hours every week. Present this in a team meeting or to a business owner and ask a simple question: "Does anyone have a task they'd want me to look at and see how I could maybe automate it with AI?" Every new task collected runs through the same loop of finding the constraint, naming the metric, building the fix, and proving the result.

### Step 4: Turn Results Into Money [07:39](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vY0EzTP-7EA&t=459s)
The final step is converting your case studies into income. Freelancers should start with warm contacts, use paid audits, price builds off the audit, and aim for monthly retainers to stabilize income. In-house consultants should present their stack of wins to their boss, frame every win as the boss's win, and push to make the AI role official. For job seekers, bringing a working demo into an interview makes you the AI hire companies are desperately seeking.
