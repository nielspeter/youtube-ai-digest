## TL;DR
A husband-and-wife team running the better-for-you cereal brand Three Wishes uses GPT-5.6 (Codex) as their "technical co-founder," building a custom operational command center for seasonal product launches from their dining room. The tool frees up meaningful time for brand-building and family life—something they say previously felt achievable only by large, billion-dollar companies.

## Key Takeaways
- Three Wishes is a protein-rich, low-sugar, gluten- and grain-free cereal inspired by the founders' own household needs.
- The business is a true family operation, run by just two people, which makes scaling and avoiding mistakes increasingly high-stakes.
- The founders historically felt blocked by a lack of technical expertise, repeatedly saying they needed a technical co-founder.
- Codex (powered by GPT-5.6) fills that technical co-founder role, turning disorganized voice instructions into polished, working software.
- Seasonal product launches (e.g., pumpkin spice) were previously "operational nightmares" requiring retailer-specific timelines and contacts.
- The founders prompted Codex to build a sell-in timeline tracker, instructing it to use historical launch data and Three Wishes' new brand guidelines.
- GPT-5.6 produced a branded, multi-slice dashboard after roughly five minutes of spoken direction—something GPT-5.5 could not do.
- The resulting tool feels like bespoke enterprise software, giving a small two-person team capabilities associated with billion-dollar companies.
- The biggest benefit is reclaimed time, which the founders can redirect toward brand-building or being with their children.

With 5.6, about five minutes of spoken direction yielded all the data slices the team needed, matched to brand guidelines.

**[01:34] — Enterprise-grade software and reclaimed family time**
