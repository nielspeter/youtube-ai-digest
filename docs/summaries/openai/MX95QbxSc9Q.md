## TL;DR
Barak Kaufman of Wonderful describes how his company partners with OpenAI to build, manage, and optimize AI agents for the world's largest enterprises. He argues that organizations should focus on transforming how work is done rather than building AI infrastructure themselves, and that we are entering an era of longer-horizon, multi-agent, multi-modal systems that can fundamentally redesign enterprise productivity.

## Key Takeaways
- Wonderful is an enterprise applied AI partner serving the world's largest and most complex organizations.
- OpenAI's Codex was instrumental in helping Wonderful's engineering team build their platform extremely fast.
- Wonderful's platform is designed to build, manage, and optimize AI agents, paired with a local forward-deploy delivery team to get agents into production.
- Enterprises moving fastest recognize they are not in the business of building AI infrastructure—they are in the business of transforming their organization with AI.
- Companies should focus on enabling teams to move faster, including the change management required for agentic go-live.
- The industry is entering a phase of longer-horizon agentic tasks involving multiple agents.
- These new systems also involve multiple modalities of interaction.
- The goal is shifting from automating existing manual tasks to rethinking how an enterprise should work in 2026 and beyond.

Kaufman explains that the second part of the partnership involves bringing OpenAI's intelligence to enterprises worldwide. He advises that enterprises moving fastest understand they are not in the business of building AI infrastructure; instead, they focus on transforming their organizations and enabling teams through the change management needed for agentic go-live.

### Redesigning Work for 2026 and Beyond [01:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MX95QbxSc9Q&t=61s)
Kaufman describes a new phase in which longer-horizon agentic tasks are possible, typically involving multiple agents and multiple interaction modalities.
