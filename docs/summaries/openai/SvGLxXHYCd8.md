## TL;DR
Deutsche Telekom is integrating OpenAI's models into its core customer service operations across Europe, handling millions of daily customer interactions with AI-powered features like live translation, in-call assistance, and post-call summarization. The company is already seeing significant efficiency gains and meaningful containment rates, while valuing the deep collaboration and knowledge transfer with OpenAI's engineering team.

## Key Takeaways
- Deutsche Telekom handles roughly 40 million customer interactions annually across Europe, representing a massive opportunity to improve or harm customer experience.
- OpenAI models are being embedded directly into the core of customer-facing operations where customers interact millions of times per day.
- Key AI use cases include live translation, in-call assistance, and post-call summarization.
- The company is achieving containment rates of up to 50%, meaning half of interactions can be resolved without human agents.

- The working relationship with OpenAI is highly valued, with OpenAI's engineers functioning effectively as an extension of Deutsche Telekom's team.
- Knowledge transfer between OpenAI and Deutsche Telekom personnel extends beyond the end of formal projects, creating lasting internal capability.
- Leadership believes these AI platforms are on a path to ultimately outperform human agents, at least within the customer service context.
